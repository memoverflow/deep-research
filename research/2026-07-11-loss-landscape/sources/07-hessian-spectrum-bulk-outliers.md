Key finding: The spectrum of the Hessian of modern deep networks (tens of millions of parameters, trained on real data) exhibits a "bulk-and-outliers" structure — a continuous bulk centered near zero plus a small number of outlier eigenvalues, roughly equal in count to the number of classes in the classification task. Corroborates earlier small-scale findings (Sagun et al.) at production scale.
