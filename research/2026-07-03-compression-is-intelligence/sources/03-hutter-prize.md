The Hutter Prize is a cash prize funded by Marcus Hutter which rewards data compression improvements on a specific 1GB English text file (enwik9), launched 2006, with goal of encouraging AI research. Total funding 500,000 euros (expanded 10x from 50,000 in 2020, enwik8 100MB -> enwik9 1GB).

Key facts:
- Organized by Hutter, Matt Mahoney, Jim Bowery
- Prize: 5,000 euros per 1% compression improvement
- Rationale: text compression and AI are equivalent problems; Hutter proved optimal behavior of goal-seeking agent in unknown computable environment is to guess shortest consistent program (AIXI); Kolmogorov complexity not computable, so AIXItl restricted version computable in O(t^2 l) but still intractable
- 2024 winner: Kaido Orav & Byron Knoll, fx2-cmix, 110,793,128 bytes, 7,950€ award
- 2017 rule change requires open-source release of submissions
- ChatGPT-style models excluded due to compute/storage constraints of competition rules
