Uses gzip compression distance + k-nearest-neighbors for text classification with zero trained parameters. Demonstrates compression-based similarity (compressing two similar texts together yields fewer bits than dissimilar texts) can rival/beat some trained neural classifiers in low-resource settings. Direct empirical application of the "compression captures structure" principle without any learned model.
