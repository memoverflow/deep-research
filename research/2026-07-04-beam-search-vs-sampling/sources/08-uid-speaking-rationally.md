Core hypothesis: Uniform Information Density (UID) — speakers prefer to distribute
information (surprisal) as evenly as possible across an utterance, for communicative
efficiency (avoiding both boring low-information stretches and overwhelming high-information
bursts). Building on Aylett & Turk (2004) and Genzel & Charniak (2002).

Used as the theoretical basis for why human-produced text has a fairly uniform (not maximal)
per-word information/surprisal profile, contrasting with MAP-decoded text which skews toward
minimal surprisal (maximal predictability) throughout.
