Key point (from search snippet): The paper draws a connection between beam search's implicit
regularization effect and the Uniform Information Density (UID) hypothesis from cognitive
science (Levy & Jaeger, 2007) — humans prefer to distribute information evenly across an
utterance. Beam search's small-beam bias may accidentally approximate this human preference,
which is why small beams sometimes "look" more human-like than exhaustive MAP search.

Used to source the Uniform Information Density connection used to explain why MAP-optimal text
and human text diverge.
