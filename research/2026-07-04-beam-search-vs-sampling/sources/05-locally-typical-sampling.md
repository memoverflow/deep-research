Abstract: Probabilistic language generators fall short despite good perplexity — this
discrepancy is explained via an information-theoretic framing of NLG as a discrete stochastic
process. Proposes selecting words whose information content is close to the expected
information content (conditional entropy) at that decoding step — "locally typical sampling."
Outperforms nucleus/top-k in reducing degenerate repetition while maintaining quality on
summarization and story generation.

Used in article as the information-theoretic sampling method that most directly operationalizes
the Uniform Information Density hypothesis.
