Key facts (from search snippets):
- DeepSeek-R1 (a reasoning model trained with large-scale RL) is used as a *teacher* to
  produce reasoning traces (chain-of-thought data), which are then used to fine-tune/distill
  six smaller dense models based on the Qwen2.5 and Llama3 families (1.5B up to 70B
  parameters) — DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B/7B/14B/32B and Distill-Llama-8B/70B.
- The Qwen-based distilled checkpoints reportedly were fine-tuned with ~800k curated
  samples generated by DeepSeek-R1 (per Ollama model card description).
- Notably these distilled models are trained via straightforward supervised fine-tuning on
  R1-generated CoT text, not full logit-level KD (a "black-box"/sequence-level style
  distillation, matching Kim & Rush 2016's framing of sequence-level KD, just applied to
  reasoning traces instead of translations).
- This is presented as empirical evidence that distilling reasoning-heavy teacher outputs
  into much smaller dense students can transfer a surprising amount of reasoning capability,
  reigniting broad industry interest in distillation as a practical way to get "smart small
  models" cheaply.
