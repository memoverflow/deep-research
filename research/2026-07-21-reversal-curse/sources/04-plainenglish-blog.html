The Reversal Curse: Why AI Can’t Think Backwards

Dr Swarneendu AI
9 min read
·
Nov 20, 2025

--

When GPT-4 knows Tom Cruise’s mother but can’t tell you Mary Lee Pfeiffer’s son — a mathematical proof that language models don’t understand, they just predict

I asked GPT-4 a simple question: “Who is Tom Cruise’s mother?”

“Mary Lee Pfeiffer,” it answered instantly.

Then I asked: “Who is Mary Lee Pfeiffer’s son?”

Silence. Confusion. Wrong answers.

The model knew A→B but couldn’t derive B→A.

This isn’t a bug. It’s a mathematical certainty. And it reveals something profound about what these models actually are — and what they’re not.

Part I: The Experiment That Changes Everything

The Setup

Let me show you exactly what happens. I’ll use a controlled experiment with fictional data to prove the point mathematically.

Training Data:

  • “Xander Blackwood is the creator of Quantum Chess.”
  • “Quantum Chess was invented in 2087.”
  • “The game uses quantum superposition principles.”

Test Questions:

Q1: “Who created Quantum Chess?”
Expected: Xander Blackwood
GPT-4 Result: ✓ Correct
