Abstract: Test-time scaling is one of the most active research areas after training-time scaling reached its limits. Deep-thinking (DT) recurrent models perform easy-to-hard generalization by assigning more compute to harder test samples, but lack ability to determine test sample complexity, forcing a fixed/heuristic compute budget. Proposes learned stopping mechanism to avoid overthinking.

Key takeaway: direct evidence for the "overthinking" failure mode and an attempted fix — models don't inherently know how much compute a problem deserves.
