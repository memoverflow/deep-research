Notes from search snippets: LLMs apply fixed inference-time compute regardless of task complexity, often overthinking simple problems while underthinking hard ones. Long reasoning models (o1, R1) exhibit "underthinking" — frequently switching between reasoning thoughts without sufficiently exploring promising paths to reach correct conclusions, i.e. abandoning good lines of reasoning too early on genuinely hard problems.

Key takeaway: overthinking (simple problems) and underthinking (hard problems) are two sides of the same "models can't judge problem difficulty" failure mode — directly supports the "boundaries" section of the article.
