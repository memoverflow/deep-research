## Key content
Identifies the "representation degeneration problem": word embeddings learned via likelihood maximization with weight tying, especially on large training sets, tend to degenerate and distribute into a narrow cone, limiting representational power. This phenomenon is known in later literature as "anisotropy" — hidden representations end up unexpectedly close to each other in cosine similarity / angular distance regardless of semantic content.

## Notes for article
Used as a parallel/related phenomenon to rank collapse — same underlying "representation space gets compressed" pathology, manifesting at the embedding layer rather than deep in the attention stack.
